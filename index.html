Frequently Asked Questions

What makes a strong password?

A strong password should be long (12+ characters), use a mix of uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols, and avoid common words or patterns. Random combinations are much harder to crack than meaningful phrases or personal information.

Why use a password generator?

Using unique, complex passwords for each account is essential for security. A password generator helps create strong passwords that are difficult to crack or guess. Humans tend to create predictable patterns even when trying to be random, which is why an automated generator provides significantly better security.

How often should I change my passwords?

While practices vary, it's generally recommended to change passwords for critical accounts (banking, email, etc.) every 3-6 months. For less sensitive accounts, changing once a year is sufficient if you're using strong, unique passwords. Always change passwords immediately if you suspect a breach or compromise.
